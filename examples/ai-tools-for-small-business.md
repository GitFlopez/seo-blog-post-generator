**H2: 6. Tidio — AI Customer Service That Doesn't Sleep**

Missing customer inquiries because you're busy running the business is a real revenue problem. **Tidio** is an AI chatbot you can add to your website in 20 minutes.

It handles:
- Common customer questions (hours, pricing, return policy)
- Lead capture when you're offline
- Order status queries
- Appointment booking

The AI learns from your website content and past conversations. You only step in for complex issues. For a business with 20+ customer inquiries per week, Tidio pays for itself quickly.

**H2: 7. Descript — Video and Podcast Editing by Deleting Text**

If you do any video marketing, client testimonials, or podcast content, **Descript** is the AI tool that removes the biggest time sink: editing.

Descript transcribes your video/audio automatically and lets you edit it like a Google Doc — delete a sentence from the transcript, and it's cut from the video. No timeline scrubbing.

Additional AI features:
- Remove filler words ("um", "uh") automatically
- Fix eye contact in talking-head videos
- Studio Sound: remove background noise in one click

For a business owner doing their own video content, this cuts 2-3 hours of editing down to 20 minutes. [INTERNAL LINK: video marketing for small business]

**Q: What are the best AI tools for small business in 2026?**
**A:** The best AI tools for small business in 2026 include Claude (writing and content), Canva AI (design), ChatGPT (research and drafting), Notion AI (operations), and Zapier (automation). Most have free tiers, making them accessible for businesses under 10 employees without a large tech budget.

For small business owners just starting with AI, Claude and Canva AI are the highest-impact first tools — they address the two biggest time drains: content creation and visual design.

---

**Q: How much do AI tools for small business cost?**
**A:** AI tools for small business typically cost $0-50/month per tool. Most popular tools — including Claude, Canva, and ChatGPT — offer free tiers that handle basic tasks. Paid plans range from $15-30/month per tool, so a complete AI stack of 3-4 tools runs $45-100/month total.

That cost is typically offset within the first week: if these tools save 5 hours of work at even a $30/hour effective rate, the monthly cost pays for itself in hours 1-2 of time saved.

---

**Q: Can a small business owner use AI tools without technical skills?**
**A:** Yes — modern AI tools for small business require no technical skills. Tools like Claude, Canva AI, and ChatGPT work in plain English. You describe what you need, and the AI produces a result. No code, no prompting expertise, and no IT department required.

The learning curve is typically 1-2 hours per tool before you're getting consistent value. Most tools offer in-app tutorials that walk you through the first use case in under 15 minutes.

---

**Q: What's the difference between Claude and ChatGPT for small business use?**
**A:** Both Claude and ChatGPT are AI writing assistants for small business, but they have different strengths. Claude tends to produce longer, more structured content and handles nuanced instructions well — ideal for blog posts, proposals, and email sequences. ChatGPT with Browse is better for real-time research, since it can access current web information.

For most small business content tasks, either works well. Many owners use both: Claude for writing, ChatGPT for research.
